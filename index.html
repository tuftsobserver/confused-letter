Dear Newsfeed,

I hope this isn’t too forward, but I’ve been checking you out a lot lately. At least two to three times before 3 p.m. and like, eight times after dinner. Sometimes, I’ll even go to certain places in the Campus Center because they have better wifi, so I know I’ll be able to reach you faster. I downloaded you on my phone so I can spend more time with you. I desperately await each new update or notification. I’ll admit it, I’m addicted.

It wasn’t always this way. I remember, way back when, I’d sneak a peek at you two, maybe three times a day. It was nice, non-committal. I liked what I saw. You gave me the headlines I needed to know, the updates I kind of wanted, and the social validation I needed. You were a nice new addition to my life, a good source of information, a connection to a larger community. How else would I be able to stay up to date on the messed up shit that goes on in the Tufts 2019 page if it weren’t for you, Newsfeed?

I don’t know exactly when things started to change, but I think I can trace it back to one thing: election season. Things started innocently enough, a few posts about Hillary, a joke or two about how stupid it would be if Donald Trump actually secured the Republican nomination. I was intrigued, sure, but I kept my distance from you. I didn’t post anything, I threw in a like or two occasionally. I was being smart, I thought. I knew it wouldn’t be healthy to jump into things too quickly. I’ve been burned before; I wanted to protect my heart.

But then Bernie Sanders came into the picture. At first, Newsfeed, I thought you were posting photos of my Grandpa and accidentally tagging him as another old White man. It wasn’t until I watched a five-second video clip that I realized this man was not my grandfather, but was in fact a real live politician. This guy was real, trashing Wall Street and late-stage capitalism with a good-natured Brooklyn accent and unkempt hair that screamed, “I am a socialist!”

The people were #feelingthebern and I was #feelingyou, Newsfeed. You were giving me exactly what I wanted and I was eating it up. I know, I know, things weren’t perfect. That Bernie quote you posted about how Syria was just “a quagmire within a quagmire” did, admittedly, make me a little nervous, but by then it was too late. With every new anti-capitalist Bernie meme, Sarah Silverman commercial, or New York reference, you pulled me in deeper and deeper until I became lost in a blissful wonderland, full of promise and political revolution.

...I should’ve known it could never last.

As Bernie lost his spark, so did we. What happened to us? Where did that fire, that passion we had during the primaries go? And, most importantly, why aren’t we talking about it? And don’t you dare try to play dumb by notifying me of another “friendship anniversary” or introducing a new “like” button, because when you strip away all of that you can see our relationship for what it truly is: a shell of its former self.

It’s just frustrating, honestly. I don’t even know why I’m still in this relationship at this point. I give and give my time to you constantly, and what do I get in return? The same sexist Hillary posts and ignorant statements by sad, confused White people who refuse to acknowledge their privilege. Just when I log on thinking you’re going to give me something good, I just read the same, reproduced shit with a slightly different Trump quote that makes my ovaries want to shrivel and die.

I’m tired, Newsfeed. I’m tired of the way things are going and I’m tired of the way you’ve been treating me. I deserve the same respect that Donald Trump gives to cis-White heterosexual males who also happen to be billionaires. No, scratch that, I want more than respect. I want to be courted, wooed. I want to be pursued like those big Wall Street donors that Hillary goes after. And if you can’t do that? Well, there’s only about a month away from Election Day. If I don’t see you trying to make some serious improvements to this relationship by then…

Instagram’s been looking real good these days after that new update.

With love (but only occasionally),

Katherine Alpert Hirsch
